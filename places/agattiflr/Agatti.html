A Coral Isle

Agatti Island, situated approximately 459 km (248 nautical miles) west of Kochi, is nestled between the enchanting waters of the Indian Ocean. Its coordinates lie between 10o 48′ and 10o 53′ N latitude and 72o 09′ and 7o 13′ E longitude, covering an area of 3.84 sq km. With a maximum length of 10 km and a width that varies, the island presents a captivating north-east to south-west orientation, extending into a graceful tail towards the south

Mode of Transport Information
Agatti and Bangaram islands can be reached by flight from Kochi. Indian Airlines operate flights from Kochi. Onward flights from Kochi are available to most of the airports in India and abroad. Airstrip is there in Agatti island only. From Agatti boats are available to Kavaratti and Kadmat during fair season October to May. Helicopter transfer is available from Agatti to Kavaratti during monsoon season subjected to availability of helicopter. The flight from Cochin to Agatti takes approximately one hour and thirty minutes
There is no rail connectivity to Lakshadweep, as it is an island, 300 kms off the coast of Kerala.
There aren’t much transportation facilities available to travel around, once you have reached the island. The reason being, limited road connectivity and in some islands, there is no road connectivity at all. You may either hire a bicycle or travel around the island on foot.
Since Lakshadweep is a group of islands, there is no road connectivity. One of the ways to travel in and around Lakshadweep is by boats/ ferries. Boats/ ferries can be used for getting around from one island to the other. For more information visit https://samudram.utl.gov.in/ For ship tickets visit https://lakport.utl.gov.in/
Seven passenger ships MV Kavaratti, MV Arabian Sea, MV Lakshadweep Sea, MV Lagoon, MV Corals, MV Amindivi and MV Minicoy operate between Cochin and Lakshadweep islands. The passage takes 14 to 18 hours depending on island chosen for journey. The ships offer different classes of accommodation: A/C First Class with two berth cabins, A/C Secound Class with four berth cabins and push back/Bunk Class with A/C seating. A doctor is available on call on board. MV Amindivi and MV Minicoy also offer comfortable A/C seating ideal for a night’s journey. During fair season, high speed vessels operate between islands.
